Regina Seibel

PhD candidate in Economics

University of Zurich, Department of Economics

I am a fourth-year PhD student in Economics at the University of Zurich, supervised by professor Armin Schmutzler and professor Gregory Crawford. My research interests are in the fields of industrial organization and competition policy. Primarily, I focus on exclusionary practices by dominant or collusive firms, taking into account effects on innovation and investment activity.

I completed both my bachelor’s (2016) and master’s (2017) degree in Economics at the Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität in Munich. During that time, I attended undergraduate courses at Monash University, Melbourne, and PhD courses at the University of California, Berkeley.

Work In Progress

Collusion by Exclusion in Public Procurement Auctions with Bidder Preselection

We analyze how bid-rigging cartels can exploit bidder preselection rules. Our analysis rests on theoretical predictions which we test on a unique dataset on electronic public procurement auctions in Slovakia. We show that bidder preselection allows cartels to avoid competition by competitive rivals.
Regina Seibel, Samuel Skoda
